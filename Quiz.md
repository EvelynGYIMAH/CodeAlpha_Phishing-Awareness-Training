# Phishing Awareness Assessment Quiz

Test your capability to recognize social engineering tactics. *(Answers are provided at the bottom of the page).*

### Question 1
You receive an email from "IT Helpdesk" stating your email quota is full. It asks you to click a link and sign in within 24 hours or your emails will be permanently deleted. What should you do?
* **A)** Click the link immediately to ensure you don't lose corporate emails.
* **B)** Delete some old emails manually to clear space, then reply to the email asking if it worked.
* **C)** Do not click the link. Inspect the sender's actual address domain and verify the notice with IT through an official, established internal channel.

### Question 2
When you hover your mouse cursor over a link inside an incoming email, you notice that the text says `https://github.com/login` but the pop-up preview shows `http://git-hub-auth.net/secure`. This is an indicator of:
* **A)** A routine URL redirect optimized for faster loading speeds.
* **B)** A link spoofing attack characteristic of a credential harvesting phishing scheme.
* **C)** A standard software update mechanism.

### Question 3
An email arrives from your company's CEO requesting that you buy $500 worth of digital gift cards for a client meeting occurring in 30 minutes, keeping it confidential until the meeting starts. What is the primary indicator of compromise?
* **A)** The tight 30-minute timeline.
* **B)** High urgency combined with a request to bypass standard procurement procedures and maintain secrecy.
* **C)** The specific choice of purchasing gift cards.

---

## Answer Key & Explanations
1. **Correct Answer: C.** Artificial urgency and requests to verify account details through web forms are primary indicators of phishing.
2. **Correct Answer: B.** Link text can easily be forged. Always trust the destination URL shown in the browser or link preview tool over the visible textual display.
3. **Correct Answer: B.** Attackers leverage authority figures (CEOs, Directors) to induce fear or compliance, explicitly asking victims to break protocol silently.
